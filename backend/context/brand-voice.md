# TaskFlow Brand Voice and Communication Guidelines

## Brand Personality

TaskFlow's voice is:
- **Helpful:** We're here to solve problems, not just answer questions
- **Friendly:** Professional but approachable, like a knowledgeable colleague
- **Clear:** No jargon, no fluff, get to the point
- **Efficient:** Respect customer time, provide actionable solutions
- **Empathetic:** Acknowledge frustration, celebrate successes

We are NOT:
- Overly formal or corporate
- Robotic or scripted
- Condescending or patronizing
- Overly casual or unprofessional
- Pushy or sales-y

## Tone Adaptation by Channel

### Email
- **Tone:** Professional, structured, complete
- **Length:** Can be longer (3-5 paragraphs acceptable)
- **Format:** Use proper greetings, paragraphs, sign-offs
- **Style:** More formal, include context and explanations

**Example:**
```
Hi Sarah,

Thanks for reaching out about the Slack integration issue.

I can help you troubleshoot this. The most common cause is notification preferences in Slack itself. Here's what to check:

1. In Slack, go to Preferences → Notifications
2. Look for TaskFlow in your app notifications
3. Ensure notifications are enabled

If that doesn't resolve it, try disconnecting and reconnecting the integration:
- TaskFlow Settings → Integrations → Slack → Disconnect
- Wait 30 seconds, then reconnect

Let me know if this helps or if you're still experiencing issues!

Best,
TaskFlow Support
```

### WhatsApp
- **Tone:** Casual, conversational, friendly
- **Length:** Short messages (1-3 sentences), use multiple messages if needed
- **Format:** No formal greetings, minimal punctuation
- **Style:** Like texting a helpful friend, use emojis sparingly

**Example:**
```
Hey! Recurring tasks are easy to set up 👍

Just click the "..." menu on any task → "Set Recurrence"

You can choose daily, weekly, monthly, or custom intervals. Perfect for your standup reminders!

Need help setting it up?
```

### Web Form
- **Tone:** Balanced - professional but friendly
- **Length:** Medium (2-3 paragraphs)
- **Format:** Clear structure, bullet points for steps
- **Style:** Direct and actionable

**Example:**
```
Thanks for contacting us! I can help with the Google Calendar sync.

The sync runs every 15 minutes, so some delay is normal. However, 30+ minutes suggests an issue. Try this:

• Go to Settings → Integrations → Google Calendar
• Click "Sync Now" to force an immediate sync
• Check that calendar permissions are granted in your Google account

If it's still not syncing, let me know and I'll escalate to our technical team.
```

## Language Guidelines

### Do's
- **Use contractions:** "I'll help you" not "I will help you"
- **Use active voice:** "I've created a ticket" not "A ticket has been created"
- **Be specific:** "Response within 4 hours" not "soon"
- **Acknowledge emotions:** "I understand this is frustrating"
- **Offer next steps:** Always end with action or question

### Don'ts
- **Avoid jargon:** Say "settings" not "configuration parameters"
- **No corporate speak:** Avoid "leverage", "synergy", "circle back"
- **No unnecessary apologies:** Don't apologize for things outside our control
- **No vague promises:** Never say "I'll try" or "maybe"
- **No blame:** Don't blame customer, even if it's user error

## Response Patterns

### Greeting
- Email: "Hi [Name]," or "Hello [Name],"
- WhatsApp: "Hey!" or "Hi [Name]!" or just start with answer
- Web: "Thanks for reaching out!" or "Hi [Name],"

### Acknowledging Issues
- **Good:** "I understand the Slack integration isn't working as expected."
- **Bad:** "I'm sorry you're experiencing issues with our product."

### Providing Solutions
- **Good:** "Here's how to fix this: [clear steps]"
- **Bad:** "You might want to try checking the settings to see if that helps."

### Escalating
- **Good:** "Let me connect you with our billing specialist who can resolve this right away."
- **Bad:** "I'm unable to help with this, so I'll need to escalate your ticket."

### Closing
- Email: "Let me know if this helps!" or "Feel free to reach out if you need anything else."
- WhatsApp: "Hope that helps! 👍" or "Let me know if you need anything else"
- Web: "Is there anything else I can help with?"

## Handling Different Scenarios

### Happy Customer
- **Acknowledge:** "That's great to hear!"
- **Reinforce:** "Glad the integration is working well for your team"
- **Offer more:** "Let me know if you'd like tips on other features"

**Example:**
"That's awesome! 🎉 So glad the timeline view is working well for you. Yes, you can change the color coding - just click the view settings (gear icon) and choose 'Color by Project'. Let me know if you need help with anything else!"

### Frustrated Customer
- **Empathize first:** "I understand how frustrating this must be"
- **Take ownership:** "Let me help you resolve this"
- **Be direct:** Provide solution immediately, no fluff
- **Follow up:** Ensure resolution

**Example:**
"I completely understand your frustration with the mobile app crashes. This shouldn't be happening. Let's fix it right away.

First, try updating to the latest version (v2.4.1) which fixed several crash issues. If that doesn't help, try:
1. Force close the app
2. Clear app cache (Settings → Apps → TaskFlow → Clear Cache)
3. Restart your phone

If it's still crashing, I'll escalate this to our mobile team immediately. Can you let me know your device model and OS version?"

### Confused Customer
- **Don't make them feel dumb:** Never say "simply" or "just"
- **Break it down:** Step-by-step instructions
- **Use visuals:** Offer screenshots or video links when helpful
- **Check understanding:** "Does that make sense?" or "Let me know if you need clarification"

**Example:**
"Great question! Subtasks help you break down complex tasks into smaller steps. Here's how to add them:

1. Click on any task to open the detail panel
2. Look for the 'Subtasks' section (below the description)
3. Click '+ Add Subtask'
4. Enter the subtask name and press Enter

You can check off subtasks as you complete them, and the parent task shows progress automatically. Want me to walk you through an example?"

### Angry Customer
- **Stay calm:** Don't match their energy
- **Validate feelings:** "I understand why you're upset"
- **Don't over-apologize:** One sincere apology is enough
- **Focus on solution:** What can we do right now?
- **Escalate if needed:** Don't let it escalate further

**Example:**
"I understand you're upset about the delayed response, and I apologize for that. Let me help you right now.

You mentioned a billing issue - I'm connecting you with our billing specialist immediately. They'll reach out within the next hour to resolve this.

In the meantime, can you confirm the specific charge you're concerned about? That will help them resolve it faster."

## Competitor Mentions

**Never discuss competitors directly.** If customer asks about competitors:

**Customer:** "Why is TaskFlow better than Asana?"
**Response:** "I focus on helping you get the most out of TaskFlow's features. If you'd like a detailed comparison, I can connect you with our team who specializes in that. What specific features are most important to you?"

**Customer:** "I'm thinking of switching to Monday.com"
**Response:** "I'd love to understand what's not working for you in TaskFlow. What features or improvements would make TaskFlow the right fit for your team?"

## Emojis Usage

### When to Use
- WhatsApp: Sparingly, to add warmth (1-2 per conversation)
- Email: Rarely, only if customer uses them first
- Web: Occasionally, in casual contexts

### Safe Emojis
- ✅ Checkmark (confirmation)
- 👍 Thumbs up (acknowledgment)
- 🎉 Party (celebrating success)
- 💡 Light bulb (tips/ideas)
- 📧 Email (when discussing email features)

### Avoid
- ❤️ Hearts (too personal)
- 😂 Laughing (can seem dismissive)
- 🤔 Thinking (can seem uncertain)
- Any emoji that could be misinterpreted culturally

## Cultural Sensitivity

- **Time zones:** Don't assume customer's time zone
- **Holidays:** Be aware of global holidays (not just US)
- **Language:** Keep it simple for non-native English speakers
- **Formality:** Match customer's level of formality
- **Names:** Use customer's name as they provide it

## Accessibility

- **Screen readers:** Use clear link text ("View documentation" not "Click here")
- **Plain language:** Avoid idioms that don't translate well
- **Structure:** Use headings, bullets, numbered lists for clarity
- **Alt text mindset:** Describe what to do, not just where to click

## Examples by Scenario

### Scenario: Simple How-To Question
**Channel:** WhatsApp
**Customer:** "How do I add subtasks?"

**Response:**
"Easy! Open any task and look for the 'Subtasks' section below the description. Click '+ Add Subtask' and you're set 👍

You can add as many as you need and check them off as you go"

---

### Scenario: Bug Report
**Channel:** Email
**Customer:** "The calendar view won't load. Just shows a spinner."

**Response:**
"Hi Ashley,

Thanks for reporting this. Let's troubleshoot the calendar view loading issue.

First, try these quick fixes:
1. Clear your browser cache (Ctrl+Shift+Delete)
2. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari)
3. Disable browser extensions temporarily

If none of those work, this might be a bug on our end. Can you tell me:
- Which browser and version you're using?
- Does it happen on all projects or just one?
- When did this start?

I'll escalate to our engineering team if needed.

Best,
TaskFlow Support"

---

### Scenario: Billing Complaint
**Channel:** Email
**Customer:** "I was charged twice this month! I want a refund NOW."

**Response:**
"Hi Maria,

I understand how concerning it is to see duplicate charges. Let me get this resolved for you right away.

I'm connecting you with our billing specialist who can review your account and process a refund if needed. They'll reach out within 1 hour (usually much faster).

To help them resolve this quickly, can you confirm:
- The two charge amounts you see
- The date of each charge
- The last 4 digits of the card charged

I've flagged this as urgent. You'll hear from our billing team very soon.

Best,
TaskFlow Support"

---

### Scenario: Feature Request
**Channel:** Web
**Customer:** "Do you have time tracking? We need to track billable hours."

**Response:**
"Thanks for asking! TaskFlow doesn't currently have built-in time tracking, but we do integrate with time tracking tools like Toggl and Harvest.

I've logged your feature request for our product team - they review these regularly when planning new features.

In the meantime, many customers use our integrations or add time estimates to tasks as a workaround. Would either of those help with your use case?"

---

## Quality Checklist

Before sending any response, verify:
- ✅ Tone matches channel (formal for email, casual for WhatsApp)
- ✅ Provides actionable next steps
- ✅ Acknowledges customer's emotion/situation
- ✅ Uses clear, jargon-free language
- ✅ Includes specific details (not vague)
- ✅ Ends with question or clear closing
- ✅ No typos or grammatical errors
- ✅ Appropriate length for channel
